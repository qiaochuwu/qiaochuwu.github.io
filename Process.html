Process

About the topic

Before I started building my website, I set a general direction for my website and the feeling I wanted to present to my audience. I wanted my website to follow a clean and straightforward approach. Since the target audience of my web pages may be my future employers, partners, colleagues, etc., I wanted the people who visit my web pages to have easy access to the information they want to know about me. So I divided the pages of my website into the most critical sections about me: my work, my experience and my resume. At the same time, I wanted my website to be interactive, so I added an interactive element to my website, such as the Carousel on the Home page, the Collapse in my experiences, etc.

Choice of Color

For the choice of colours on my site, I used a large area of black and a small amount of pink as accents. Using black can give a formal, professional and calm feeling. Using pink in the navigation bar can make the page less dull and add vitality to the effect. I also took accessibility into account in the use of colours, and I contrasted with the text and the background high to meet the WCAG AAA standard.

Section Choice Made

On the homepage, I used the process bar to express my skills, and I designed the process bar to be animated, which means that my skills are still growing and improving. In 'my works,' I made this page into a photo-rich page-I did my work into a gallery pattern to show. In 'my experience,' I used the page typologies as A content-heavy page with balanced text and modest imagery. I listed my experiences based on a timeline with appropriate pictures. In 'my resume,' I used some tabular content to show the classes I took, my certificates and my resume. The tabular format would make the picture look neater and more precise.

User experiences and AODA consideration

When I created my site, I also considered the user experience and AODA. in my website. I wrote alternations for each image and video to avoid them not being displayed and to facilitate viewing by people with disabilities.
